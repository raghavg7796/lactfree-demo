Questions

Asked, answered.

How do I know lactose is my problem?+
Most people never connect the dots. They just know chai, coffee or a glass of milk often leaves them feeling heavy or bloated, and quietly cut back. Lactose is a common reason (your doctor can confirm it). And lactose-free milk is just milk: same chai, same kitchen, nothing else changes.
Why not just oat or almond milk?+
If plant milk works for you, lovely. We built this for people who want their milk back. Three honest differences: plant drinks can split in boiling chai and hot filter coffee (the barista versions that survive cost more). A glass of milk has several times the natural protein of typical almond or oat drinks. And plant milks in India often run ₹250 to ₹600 a litre; this is real milk at ₹115. In chai or coffee it tastes the way milk always did.
Is this real milk?+
Yes — 100% real cow's milk. We add the lactase enzyme, which breaks the lactose down into simpler sugars before it reaches you. That's also what makes it easy to digest. Nothing else changes.
Why does it taste slightly sweeter?+
When lactose breaks down, it becomes glucose and galactose, which taste sweeter than lactose does. Same sugar quantity, no added sugar. In chai and coffee, most people can't tell.
Isn't A2 milk the same thing?+
No. A2 refers to the protein type in the milk. A2 milk contains just as much lactose as any other milk. Lactose-free milk is the only milk where the lactose itself is gone.
