The fine print, out loud

Protein and lactose are different parts of milk. The high-protein milks everywhere are regular dairy underneath — every gram of lactose is still in there. The ones boosted with milk solids? That can add even more.

And the few that are both high-protein and lactose-free come as 250ml zero-fat packs at ₹300–400 a litre. Great after a workout. You can't run a kitchen on them.

We're building the third thing: one honest jug — high protein and zero lactose.

Questions

Asked, answered.

How much protein, exactly?+
The final grams go on the pack at launch. We're building for meaningfully more protein per glass than regular toned milk — and we won't print a number until the recipe is locked and tested. That's a promise, not a dodge.
Will it taste like those protein milks?+
We know the ones you mean. When milk gets loaded with protein powders, it turns chalky, tastes cooked, and falls apart in hot tea. So we're not chasing a giant number — we're building a moderate boost that still behaves like milk. If it can't make a proper chai and set curd, it doesn't ship.
Isn't regular protein milk the same thing?+
No. Protein and lactose are different parts of milk — regular high-protein milk keeps all its lactose (and protein added as milk solids can raise it further). LactFree is engineered for both at once: more protein AND zero lactose.
Is this real milk?+
Yes — 100% real cow's milk. We add the lactase enzyme, which breaks the lactose down into simpler sugars before it reaches you.
Why does it taste slightly sweeter?+
When lactose breaks down, it becomes glucose and galactose — which taste sweeter than lactose does. Same sugar quantity, no added sugar. In chai and coffee, most people can't tell.
